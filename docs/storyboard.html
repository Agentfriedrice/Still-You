STILL YOU

storyboard & system flowchart
An interactive 2D pixel-art web experience where the user walks through a dark, memory-like hallway, encountering objects from the past, and makes small choices that shape a reflective ending about growth, regret, care, and identity.

Title Screen

The very first thing the player sees. Sets the mood before any hallway exists. A single softly-glowing window foreshadows the architecture; the title and subtitle fade in over a few seconds; "press E to begin" pulses quietly at the bottom.

Opening Frame

"a memory in four hallways."
Design notes:
  • Same visual hardware as the hallways. CRT scanlines, vignette frame, drifting dust, the boxed 4:3 viewport — the title screen is rendered on the same imagined cartridge as the rest of the experience.
  • Cream window foreshadows Year 4. The single warm-cream window in the center uses the Year 4 "becoming" palette, not any of the earlier years. The journey starts and ends in the same light — the title is a quiet preview of where the player is going.
  • Staged fade-in. Black for ~0.8s, then title fades in (~0.5s delay, 1.8s tween), then subtitle (~2s delay), then controls (~3.3s delay, dim), then "press E to begin" pulses in last (~3.8s delay). Total wait: ~3.8s before the prompt is visible. Input is live from the start though — the staging is mood, not gating.
  • Press E (or click). The title fades to black over ~1.1s and the first hallway loads. From this point on the title screen only returns once: after the epilogue hug, at the very end.

The Arc

Title screen, four hallways, four years, and a quiet epilogue. Each hallway ends in a doorway; the doorway reflects what the player did and lets them step through to the next.

Visual Language

A 4:3 boxed frame used to invoke a sense of nostalgia. Side-scrolling hallway with windows along the back wall, floor tiles, soft overhead lamp pools, drifting dust particles, and a CRT scanline overlay. These elements allow the experience to feel like an old game cartridge that remembers you.
Common elements (used for every year):
  • 4:3 boxed viewport — containment. Memories arrive framed.
  • CRT scanlines + thin vignette frame — nostalgia, an old screen vibe.
  • Windows along the back wall — framed glass with a cross mullion and a sill, with a faint wash of the year's ambient light over the pane and a soft spill of light below. The hallway reads as somewhere, not nowhere — and the windows double as anchors for the Year 4 "stay a while" sun-ray effect when the player sits in the chair.
  • Overhead lamp pools — soft elliptical light. Anchors the player to the architecture.
  • Floor tile lines — short stripes at the front edge of the floor; reads as perspective.
  • Drifting dust particles — tinted by the year's palette. Slow, ambient motion.
  • Memory glows — soft colored ellipse under each interactable. Brightens after a choice.
  • "?" → "·" — the hint above each memory swaps to a quiet dot once the player has chosen.
  • Doorway threshold — a warm vertical line in a faint frame at the right edge, with a small "step through" hint when ready.
  • Per-year outfit shift — the player's hair, skin, and eyes stay constant across all four years (that's the "you" that persists). Torso, collar, and legs re-tint each year to sit inside the new colorwave: blue/gray → muted green → amber/rust → warm cream.

  • "You walked four hallways."

Epilogue — Stay a While

The closing beat that only the player who chose [Q] "stay a little longer" at the final doorway will see. After sitting in the chair and watching the sun fill the room, the player can stand and walk back through every hallway — now illuminated — and meet their first-year self at the start of Year 1.

Epilogue — The Walk Back

"go back through the hallways, if you like."
Trigger: The player picks [Q] stay a little longer at the Year 4 final doorway. A chair appears beside the windows. Sitting in it fills the hallway with warm light over ~20 seconds, and silently sets epilogueMode = true. After the sun has fully settled (~21s), a quiet italic line fades in at the bottom of the viewport: "go back through the hallways, if you like."

The walk back

FORWARD DOORWAYSSealed in epilogue mode. The right-edge "step through" hint hides — the player has already passed that point.
BACK DOORWAYSA new threshold appears on the LEFT edge of Years 2/3/4 — same beam + frame geometry as the forward doorways, just facing the other way. Walking into it transitions back to the previous year.
ILLUMINATIONEvery hallway loads with the warm Year 4 sun layer over its own palette (Year 3 keeps its amber, Year 2 keeps its green, etc.). The colors stay; the light is the constant. Fast 1.5s fade-in so the room is bright by the time the player walks in.
MEMORIESGlows still visible with their permanent "·" marks. Pressing E next to one does nothing. The past is past — looking is allowed, re-deciding is not.
CAPThe graduation cap (if worn or kept) rides back through every hallway. Visually the older self walks toward their first-year self wearing the original blue/gray outfit AND the cap they earned — the past and the journey together.

First-year self · "It's you!" · hug

PAST-SELFSpawns at x=200 (the player's original spawn) in Year 1 in epilogue mode. Same composite sprite as the player, locked to the original Year 1 outfit. Soft footprint glow underneath; quiet "?" floating above. No radiating halo — the silhouette stands alone.
DIALOGUEApproach + [E] opens a single-choice conversation that bypasses the regular memory dialogue machine:
   "It's you.
   Smaller than you remembered.
   Carrying everything you didn't know yet."
[E] →
   "What do you do?
   > hug"
[E] → the hug.
HUGBoth walk toward each other (~1.4s). The player tweens ~6px UPWARD as they approach so the older self stands visibly taller in the embrace — backing up "smaller than you remembered." The past-self leans into the older self (rotation 0.12) and HOLDS the lean for 4 seconds — the embrace is the still moment, not an animated one. No radiating glow.
WHISPERAt t≈4.5s, the final line fades in over the embrace: "you were always going to make it." Holds for a few seconds while the lean unwinds.
CLOSINGAt t≈9s, the camera fades to black over 3.5s. All per-playthrough state resets, then scene.start("TitleScene"). The title screen returns. The game can be played again.
One-time use, by design. The first-year self only exists in epilogue mode. A player who picks [E] continue at the final doorway never sees them. The chair, the walk back, the hug, the whisper — these are entirely earned by the [Q] stay a little longer choice. The game doesn't advertise them.

System Flowchart

Per-frame loop on top, dialogue state machine and memory model in the middle, multi-hallway flow next, then the EPILOGUE PATH (warm yellow) showing the [Q] "stay a little longer" branch — chair → walk back → first-year self → hug → fade to title. The TitleScene bubble sits above Year 1 and is the loop-back target for the epilogue. Each year box lists the world palette and the matching outfit re-tint. The ENDING node is two-stage: a per-year recap line drawn from each year's doorwayReflections, then a cumulative closing line drawn from Year 4's separate finalReflections bucket. Dashed gray arcs are [Q] backtracking; the dashed white arc is the [E] restart from the final doorway; the dashed warm arcs are the epilogue path and its loop-back to the title.
